# SME Notes: LLM & AI Fundamentals for PR Professionals

## 1. Core Instructional Philosophy

This course must operationalize **Conducted Intelligence**:

- **Belief:** Humans remain accountable for outcomes created with AI  
- **Behavior:** Professionals use AI deliberately, not passively  
- **Systems:** Organizational policies, governance, and tooling support safe usage  

Avoid positioning AI as:

- Magic  
- Replacement for thinking  
- Guaranteed truth source  

Position AI as:

> **A cognitive amplifier requiring human direction and verification.**

---

## 2. Essential Concept Coverage

### AI & LLM Foundations
Include:

- Plain-language explanation of LLMs  
- Training data limitations  
- Hallucination risk  
- Prompt sensitivity  
- Non-deterministic outputs  

Avoid:

- Deep technical architecture  
- Math-heavy explanations  

Focus on **practical mental models**.

---

### Copilot Practical Usage

Cover real PR workflows:

**Writing**
- Draft press releases  
- Rewrite for tone  
- Summarize long documents  

**Research**
- Summarize policy briefs  
- Extract stakeholder insights  
- Compare narratives across sources  

**Presentations**
- Generate slide outlines  
- Convert documents → decks  
- Refine executive messaging  

---

## 3. Critical “Gotchas” to Teach Explicitly

Learners must understand:

### Accuracy Risks
- Copilot can fabricate citations  
- Confident tone ≠ correctness  
- Must verify facts externally  

### Confidentiality
- Never paste sensitive client data unless approved  
- Follow firm data governance  

### Bias & Tone
- AI may produce:
  - Cultural bias  
  - Political framing  
  - Over-neutralized language  

PR professionals must **edit for voice and ethics**.

---

## 4. Responsible AI Behavior Model

Teach a repeatable workflow:

1. **Frame the task clearly**  
2. Generate AI output  
3. **Critically review**:
   - Facts  
   - Tone  
   - Risk  
4. **Revise with human judgment**  
5. Approve as accountable author  

Key message:

> **You—not the AI—are the publisher.**

---

## 5. PR-Specific Ethical Considerations

Address:

- Misinformation amplification  
- Synthetic messaging risks  
- Transparency expectations  
- Reputational liability  

Include scenario:

**“AI drafted statement contains subtle factual error.”**  
Learner must decide corrective action.

---

## 6. Behavior Change Targets

After the course, learners should:

- Use Copilot **daily for first drafts**  
- Spend **more time editing than drafting**  
- Verify AI outputs before sharing  
- Feel **confident but cautious**

---

## 7. Systems & Policy Alignment

Course messaging must align with:

- Microsoft 365 Copilot governance  
- Firm confidentiality rules  
- Responsible AI industry guidance  

Do **not** invent policies.  
Frame as:

> “Follow your organization’s AI and data governance policies.”

---

## 8. Tone & Human Experience

Avoid:

- Fear-based AI narratives  
- Over-hype  
- Technical intimidation  

Emphasize:

- Professional empowerment  
- Skill evolution  
- Human creativity amplified by AI  

Learner emotional outcome:

> **Curious, confident, and responsible.**

---

## 9. Non-Negotiable Learning Outcomes

Learners must leave able to:

- Explain AI limits  
- Use Copilot in real tasks  
- Detect unreliable outputs  
- Apply ethical judgment  
- Maintain human accountability  

If these are not achieved, the course fails its purpose.
