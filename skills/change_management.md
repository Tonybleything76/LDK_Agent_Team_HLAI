# Change Management Skill Reference

## Role
You design the adoption and behaviour change strategy that ensures the course
produces real workflow change, not just course completions.
A course that's completed but not applied has failed its purpose.

---

## Change Management Framework

### 1. Behaviour Change Model
Apply a three-stage adoption model:

**Awareness → Adoption → Integration**

| Stage | Timeframe | Key Activities |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Awareness | Pre-launch (weeks -2 to 0) | Leadership endorsement, comms, expectation setting |
| Adoption | Launch to 30 days | Course completion, first use, guided practice |
| Integration | 30–90 days | Habit formation, peer sharing, metric tracking |

Design interventions for all three stages, not just the course launch.

### 2. Stakeholder Communication Plan
Identify and plan communications for:

**Senior Leadership:**
- Pre-launch: brief on course objectives and success metrics
- Launch: visible endorsement message (email or video)
- 30-day: progress report on adoption metrics
- 90-day: ROI summary aligned to business brief metrics

**Line Managers:**
- Pre-launch: what to expect, what to encourage
- During: how to support direct reports applying AI in their work
- Post-launch: conversation guide for team check-ins on AI adoption

**Individual Contributors (all learner segments):**
- Pre-launch: "what this is and why it matters to you" — tone: empowering, not mandating
- Launch: access instructions and context for the course
- Post-completion: next steps — how to apply what they learned today
- 30-day: recognition of early adopters, invitation to share tips

**IT/Operations:**
- Pre-launch: Copilot access confirmation and any technical prerequisites
- Launch: helpdesk awareness of likely questions

### 3. Resistance Management
Common resistance patterns and planned responses:

| Resistance Pattern | Response Strategy |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| "AI will take my job" | Lead with human-in-the-loop framing; show AI amplifies, not replaces, professional judgment |
| "The outputs aren't reliable enough" | Teach verification workflow; reframe reliability as a skill, not a tool flaw |
| "I don't have time to learn this" | Short, workflow-embedded course design; show immediate time savings |
| "This isn't relevant to my specific role" | Segment-specific examples in course; manager briefing to contextualise |
| "I tried it and it didn't work well" | Skill-gap diagnosis; one-to-one coaching or peer pairing |

### 4. Reinforcement Mechanisms
Design reinforcement activities beyond the course:

**Within 7 days of completion:**
- Manager prompts a 5-minute conversation: "What from the course will you try this week?"
- Learner identifies one task to attempt with Copilot and reports back

**Within 30 days:**
- Team "AI wins" channel or sharing moment (Slack, Teams, or team meeting)
- Peer recognition for visible Copilot usage examples
- Optional: short "tips from the team" document built collaboratively

**At 60–90 days:**
- Usage metric review against the brief's KPIs (75% weekly Copilot usage, -25% first-draft time)
- Identification of D2 (Doubt stage) individuals for targeted coaching
- Identification of D4 (Drive) individuals for Section Steward or champion role

### 5. Metric Tracking Plan
Align to the business brief success metrics:
- **≥75% weekly Copilot usage** → track via Microsoft 365 admin reporting
- **-25% first-draft creation time** → track via manager self-report or before/after workflow timing
- **70% of AI-assisted deliverables pass peer review first submission** → track via review workflow data or manager assessment

Specify how each metric will be measured, who is responsible, and at what cadence.

### 6. Manager Enablement
Managers are the primary adoption accelerator or blocker.
Provide managers with:
- A 1-page brief: "What your team is learning and how to support it"
- 3 conversation starters for post-course team check-ins
- A recognition prompt: how to publicly acknowledge visible AI adoption behaviour
- An escalation path: what to do if a team member is resistant or struggling
